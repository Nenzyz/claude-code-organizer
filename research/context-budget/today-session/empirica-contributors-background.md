A single popular Reddit/HN post can easily generate 50-80 stars in a day.

**Second spike (Feb 22-23): ~43 stars in 2 days**
- Similar pattern. Likely another Reddit post or listing (around this time Empirica appeared on LobeHub Skills Marketplace).
- **Verdict: Likely organic** — another promotion wave.

**Overall pattern:**
- Two clear promotional spikes, then long tails of 1-2 stars/day.
- This is a **normal open-source star pattern** for a niche tool with periodic promotion.
- **No signs of bought stars** (bought stars typically come in steady streams of exactly N per day, often from accounts with no repos/activity).
- The stargazers I checked have normal-looking GitHub profiles with varied creation dates and activity levels.

### Conclusion on Stars
**The 195 stars appear organic**, driven by two social media promotion waves (likely Reddit/HN). The project has real but modest adoption — 195 stars with only ~8-10 external users filing issues suggests most stargazers bookmarked but didn't deeply adopt.

### Concerns
- **Essentially a 1-person project** — David does ~95%+ of commits, with pschwinger as the only sustained contributor
- **Very high commit velocity with AI co-authorship** — raises questions about code quality vs. quantity
- **Scope creep:** 150+ CLI commands, epistemic vectors, calibration, sentinel gating, etc. for a project with <200 stars
- **Heavy self-promotion:** David posts about Empirica on Reddit (r/ClaudeAI, r/AISystemsEngineering), Twitter, Medium, DEV.to
- **Philosophical/academic tone** may limit adoption — terms like "noetic RAG", "epistemic vectors", "praxic phases" are unusual for developer tools
- **No major company or institution backing**
- **0 followers on GitHub** — despite 195 stars, nobody follows the developer personally

### Overall Legitimacy Score: Real but Niche
The project is a genuine open-source tool built by a real developer with a long open-source history. It's not a scam or fake. But it's primarily a solo passion project with modest adoption, heavy AI-assisted development, and ambitious scope relative to its actual user base.
